Project Creator

American Yawp MS was created and is currently maintained by a history educator committed to making high-quality, free history education accessible to all students.

Want to Contribute?

This project needs collaborators! We're looking for teachers, historians, curriculum designers, special education specialists, translators, designers, and students who want to help build something that matters. Visit the GitHub repository to get involved.

Special Thanks

The American Yawp — This project would not exist without the groundbreaking work of Joseph L. Locke, Ben Wright, and the over 300 historians who built the original college-level textbook. Their commitment to open access and rigorous, inclusive history education made this adaptation possible.

Stanford University Press — For publishing The American Yawp and supporting open-source scholarship.

The Creative Commons Community — For building the legal framework that makes projects like this possible.

How to Join

Contributions of all kinds are welcome:

  • Writing or reviewing chapter content
  • Designing lessons, activities, and assessments
  • Improving accessibility (translations, audio narration, readability)
  • Testing materials in real classrooms and providing feedback
  • Fixing typos, broken links, or formatting issues

Visit the GitHub repository to open an issue, submit a pull request, or just say hello.
