Built-In Pedagogical Features

Every chapter in American Yawp MS includes teaching-ready features designed to engage students and promote critical thinking:

  • Big Questions — Essential questions that frame each chapter
  • Vocabulary Boxes — Key terms defined in student-friendly language
  • Stop and Think Questions — Analytical prompts embedded throughout the text
  • Primary Source Voices — Historical documents with context
  • Multiple Perspectives Boxes — Competing viewpoints on contested events
  • "Story Behind the Story" Callouts — Engaging details that make history memorable
  • "Whose Voices Were Left Out?" Sections — Explicit attention to marginalized perspectives
  • Chapter Activities — Evidence-based discussions, debates, and rankings

Suggested Teaching Approaches

Use the "Big Questions" as anchors. Start each chapter by introducing the Big Questions. Revisit them at the end to assess understanding.

Turn "Stop and Think" questions into pair-share discussions. Have students discuss these questions with a partner before sharing with the class.

Use chapter activities as formative assessments. The activities are designed to reveal what students understand and what they're still wrestling with.

Leverage primary sources for document-based questions. The primary source boxes can be used for DBQ practice or as writing prompts.

Build on "Whose Voices Were Left Out?" Ask students to research additional perspectives or create their own "Whose Voices Were Left Out?" entries for their local communities.

Using This in Your Classroom

American Yawp MS is designed to be flexible. You can:

  • Assign chapters as independent reading
  • Project chapters on a screen for whole-class reading
  • Print individual sections for small-group work
  • Use chapters as the foundation for inquiry-based units
  • Adapt and remix chapters to fit your curriculum (under CC BY-SA 4.0)

Because this project is open-source, you're free to customize it for your students' needs. Add your own activities, rewrite sections, create translations, or build multimedia companions. The only requirement is that you share your adaptations under the same license, ensuring they remain free for others.
