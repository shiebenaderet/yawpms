The Primary Source Reader gives students direct access to the voices and evidence of the past. Each chapter includes historical documents, images, and artifacts — carefully selected and annotated for middle school readers.

For every source, students practice four skills of historical thinking: sourcing, contextualization, close reading, and corroboration.

What You'll Find

  • Historical documents — Letters, speeches, laws, and declarations
  • Visual sources — Paintings, photographs, political cartoons, and maps
  • Material culture — Artifacts, architecture, and objects from the past
  • Student-friendly annotations — Context, vocabulary, and guiding questions
  • Alignment with chapters — Sources organized to match the textbook's structure
